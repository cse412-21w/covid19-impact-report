We are going to do exploratory data analysis on the datasets related to cases of Covid-19 and economy (or any other fields impacted). By using maps/symbol maps, we will create visualizations to show the cases in different states, and by making it an interactive data visualization, we will display the changing over time. Furthermore, we will research and link relatable variables impacted with specific columns of the conditions of coronavirus, and try our best to build intuitive stories with reasonable design elements, such as positions, shapes, sizes, and colors.

Our project is intended to show the impact of COVID-19 from various aspects. It will start from an overview of cases ongoing in each state, then analyzing the impact of the COVID-19 in general. We will focus on the data of the disease (confirmed, recovered, etc) and economy (unemployment, price index, etc).

Fluctuation of Consumer Price Index during Pandemic:

The bar graph which is shown below describes the immediate impact of COVID-19 Cases on one of the economic indicators: the Consumer Price Index. In the graph, note that there is a drastic decrease of the index, represented by the bar has a negative value highlighted in red color. This happened just after when the COVID-19 became a serious case in the United States so that we can conclude that the pandemic indeed has an impact on this economic indicator.

U.S. Economy Performing in 2020:

The above plot displays the trend of three key economic indicators from Jan 2020 to Dec 2020. At the beginning of 2020, both exports and personal consumption remained stable until February. Then, they both plunged and hit the lowest point between April and May. Exports and personal consumption are two main components of the overall output. And the drastic shrinkage in output level could be explained by the simultaneous negative demand and supply shocks due to the upsurge in Covid-19 cases. Despite the rise in both indicators after May, both of them did not recover to the original level as in January.
